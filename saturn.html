Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the solar system, famous for its magnificent ring system. These rings, made of ice, rock, and dust particles, are one of the most striking features in the solar system, stretching over 280,000 kilometers in diameter but only about 10 meters thick. Saturn is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and has no solid surface. Its atmosphere displays bands of clouds and powerful storms, with wind speeds reaching up to 1,800 kilometers per hour. Saturn has at least 145 known moons, with Titan being the largest and one of the most intriguing. Titan has a dense atmosphere and surface lakes of liquid methane and ethane, making it a focus of astrobiological research.
